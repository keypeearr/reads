# Meditations by Marcus Aurelius

## Page 1 - 10
- To be decent and mild tempered
- To have integrity and manliness
- To be kind and simple
- To invest in knowledge and education
- To be mind one's own business and choose no sides in pointless quarrels
- To avoid empty enthusiams and being mindful when conversing with others
- To better the self, ready to listen to feedbacks and not allow any views of others deter one's growth
- To be morally free, devoided of belief in fortune, resilient, and self-sufficient
- To live life according to nature, uncompromised dignity
- To avoid interrupting people upon their errors and instead provide a transformative feedback
- To understand suspicion, caprice, and hypocrisy in the context of absolute rule
- To never write 'I am too busy' or avoidance of our relationships
- To never reject a person's criticism, and praise one's teachers
- To be just
- To have self-mastery and an unwavering character
- To be gentle, modest, agreeable, and enjoy life without pride or apology
- To be grateful for to the Gods and the people around
- Other poeple should not affect one's own emotions and cognitive abilities

## Page 11 - 20
- Everything happens in providence of the gods, in nature. Be grateful instead of always complaining
- Do not wait for the perfect opportunity to act
- Be mindful of one's actions with accordance to the gods
- Do not entrust your life and soul to others
- Focus on one's actions and do not drift away from what is good
- Be true to oneself
- Lust is heavier than anger due to lust being submission to pleasure while anger is unattended pain and involuntary spasm
- One's life can be easily taken away, practice gratitude to the gods who wish no harm for humankind
- One's life is quick and temporary, consider the practices and objects we like and love
- Everything has been of the same kind and it is only the present that one could be deprived, and if he/she loses it, then one cannot lose what does not possess
- All is as thinking makes it so
- Ways that one can hurt their soul:
    1. When one loses himself
    2. When it turns away from a human being
    3. When it gives to pleasure or pain
    4. When it lies
    5. When it stops being mindful of its actions
- Philosophy is what can train one's body, mind, and soul
- One's comprehension and abilities will fade sooner than one's own life
- Appreciate the beauty of nature
- We will inevitably return to our origin
- Avoid thinking about others not unless it is for common good, disregard other people's opinion and praises not unless they live in accordance to nature
- One's action must not have any conflicting motive and keeping true to his nature
- Practice self-sufficiency, and if there is something far greater than that, pursue it with your whole heart, mind and soul
- Do not compromise one's morals for expedient things
- Everyone is living in the present

## Page 21 - 30
- The mind is most powerful tool a man has. One must be able to picture things in it to indentify their nature and the necessary virtues needed for that specific thing
- Leading a good life is living full in the present with respect to one's nature
- The mind and the soul are connected. They both must be in congruence with one another
- To the body belongs sense perceptions, to the soul belongs impulses, and to the mind belongs judgements
- A person who has the body, mind, and soul in congruence lives a good and simple life. One does not have any need to defend and explain his life to others 
- Humans are capable of adapting based on their virtues and the situation at hand. No action should be taken without aim, or other than in conformity with a principle affirming the art of life
- The human mind is our way of retreating. A sacred place for us to recover and regroup. Do not allow others to tarnish and destroy this sacred place. Two things to remember: things cannot touch the mind and all these things we see will change almost as you look at them, and then will be no more. The universe is change
- Death is perplexing, it starts with combining matter and then its inevitable destruction. Why stress over what constitutes death?
- Remove the judgement, remove the thought, then it will go away
- If someone does you wrong, do not see things the way they see it or how they want you to see it. Judge them by how you see it
- Two things for readiness: do it for the common good, and be open to change as long as it is for the common good
- Our time is temporary, act with urgency, do good
- Look only at your actions and not your neighbors'
- Fame and popularity, same with life, are both temporary. One's own self-worth does not grow nor decrease with both fame and fortune, as long as one lives life in accordance to his nature
- In every impulse: give what is right. In every thought, stick to what is certain
- Removing the unnecessary frees time and energy
- Life is short so remain sober and relaxed
- Being a burden to others is being a burden to oneself
- Learn to love what you do

## Page 31 - 40
- Be open to learn from the wise
- The universe is one and everytning is connected
- Change: nothing inherently bad in the process, nothing inherently good in the result
- Nothing is permanent as time is a violent stream that will take things away
- Everthing has a cause and effect that are connected to another cause and effect
- Learn to challenge everyday situations
- There is no difference between today and tomorrow
- See the good fortune of bearing pain in dealing with misfortune
- Act in accordance of one's nature to be free of having to manage or impress others
- Why does one postpone themselves to act in accordance to their nature?
- To empty one's mind of any impression which causes trouble
- Do not revoke one's entitlement
- Do not welcome incompentency or complacency in one's life. Be open to change and willingness to grow
- Doing good should not need to be acknowledged or yield any reward
- Accept things that happens to the self
- Two reasons to be content with one's experience: this has happened to you and you alone, and it is a part of you
- Failure should not hinder one's pursuit of doing good
- Nothing happens without reason and everything is in accordance to nature and the gods
- Keep question one' purpose to live

## Page 41 - 50
- The man who deprives himself of things that are not obligated to his own life is a good man
- To prime the mind of thoughts that the soul can also absorb
- One is as capable as the universe
- Actions that others project towards man should instead be thought of as 'What harm could they have done themselves?'
- One is in control of the actions they can take upon other people's disposition towards them
- Showing gratitude to the gods rewards the soul
- Be open to discuss differences from others
- Two things common to humans: they are invulnerable to external impediments, and the good that they seek lies in the just disposition and action with their desires being the limit
- Do not let your actions bother you, if it did not bother others to begin with
- It is okay to sympathize with others, but the feelings and emotions should not carry over to you
- To do what is right is to do what is in accordance with one's nature, other people's dispositions should not be a bother nor a hinderance to one's nature
- The best revenge is not be like your enemy
- Defying the inevitable is absurd and should not be done. Instead, one should be able to accept the inevitable
- Take refuge in Philosophy and it can guide you to the right path
- To assess everything by stripping them apart naked and seeing right through the vanity
- Anything that is humanly possible, is possible
