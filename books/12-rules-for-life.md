# 12 Rules For Life by Jordan B. Peterson

## 12 Rules

- Stand up straight with your shoulders back  
- Treat yourself like someone you are responsible for helping  
- Make friends with people who want the best for you  
- Compare yourself to who you were yesterday, not to who someone else is today  
- Do not let your children do anything that makes you dislike them  
- Set your house in perfect order before you criticize the world  
- Pursue what is meaningful, not what is expedient  
- Tell the truth, or at least do not lie  
- Assume that the person you are listening to might know something you do not  
- Be precise in your speech  
- Do not bother children when they are skateboarding  
- Pet a cat when you encounter one on the street  

## Page 1–20

- Like lobsters and birds, humans value territory and social hierarchy  
- Survival of the fittest is a prevalent theme in nature  
- Nature is both static and dynamic at the same time  

## Page 21–40

- Humans are habitual beings who seek stability and predictability  
- Negative habits can disguise themselves as positive feedback loops and lead to depression or mental illness  
- Standing up for yourself creates a positive feedback loop that changes perception and outcomes  
- People often do not help themselves  
- Drama and fiction are governed by chaos, order, and consciousness  
- Chaos represents situations we cannot comprehend  

## Page 41–60

- Order represents situations that are predictable and stable  
- Archetypes such as parent, child, male, and female are integral to society  
- Order is symbolically masculine, while chaos is feminine  
- We must learn to adapt between order and chaos  
- Even fortified goodness is eventually infiltrated by evil  
- The ability of women to make men self-conscious is a primordial force  

## Page 61–80

- We often help others more than ourselves because we know our own weaknesses  
- Treat people the way they would treat you  
- Accepting less than what you offered creates tyranny and submission  
- Each person has a role in the interconnected world and must care for themselves  
- Have direction and understand your strengths and weaknesses  

## Page 81–100

- Descending is easier than ascending  
- Examine whether saving someone is for their benefit or your ego  
- Make friends with people who want the best for you  
- Someone will always be better than you; focus on self-improvement instead  

## Page 101–120

- Life is a zero-sum game; worthlessness is the default condition  
- You play many games with varying skill levels unique to you  
- The past is fixed, but the future can improve  
- Work with your inner critic to correct inadequacies  
- What you aim at determines what you perceive  
- Discipline and structure are required to pursue goals  

## Page 121–140

- Be mindful and attentive to your surroundings  
- Treat yourself better  

## Page 141–160

- Proper discipline is an act of mercy and long-term judgment  
- Ask inverse questions to reveal deeper truths  
- Correction is more effective when done sooner  

## Page 161–180

- Start small and add complexity gradually  
- Limit the number of rules  
- Use the least force necessary to enforce rules  
- Parents should operate as a pair  
- Parents must recognize their capacity for cruelty and deceit  
- Parents act as proxies for the real world  

## Page 181–200

- Many refuse to judge reality or blame existence  
- If you know something is wrong, stop doing it  
- The future is a judging father; sacrifice now for later reward  
- If reality is not what you want, reexamine your values  

## Page 201–220

- Those who desire the greatest future must sacrifice the most  
- Understanding vulnerability reveals the pain we can inflict on others  

## Page 221–240

- Philosophers differ in views on Christianity and moral consequence  
- Constraint is required before freedom and competence  
- We cannot arbitrarily create our own values  
- Ideas have aims and function like personalities  
- Expedience yields short-term gain; meaning yields compounding returns  

## Page 241–260

- Living a life-lie manipulates reality through false perception and action  
- Pride absolutizes its own creations  
- Those living a lie seek control to fix the world  
- Without revealing yourself to others, you cannot know yourself  
- Willful blindness is refusing to know what can be known  
- Inauthenticity is repeated action against known truth  
- Lies corrupt both the self and the environment  
- Honesty may not perfect the world but reduces suffering  

## Page 261–280

- A clear aim provides direction  
- Learn from past mistakes while remaining cautious of tradition  
- Learning challenges existing concepts  
- Move forward carefully while observing closely  
- Truth brings difficulty; be prepared  
- Articulating personal truth brings freedom  
- Giving advice often signals superiority  

## Page 281–300

- The present reshapes the past; the future reshapes the present  
- We impose narratives based on preconceived theories  
- Do not take responsibility for others’ problems unless asked  
- Good listeners reflect and summarize others’ perspectives  
- Allow others to validate or correct your understanding  

## Page 301–320

- Transformative listening involves mutual speaking and listening  
- Perception functions in order but shatters during chaos  
- The body reacts before conscious perception  

## Page 321–340

- Communication determines the strength of relationships  
- Allowing chaos to persist worsens disorder  
- Speaking brings order to chaos when done precisely  
- Facing fear enables accurate speech  
- Words influence past, present, and future  

## Page 341–360

- Not all good intentions are genuine  
- Data suggests women prefer equal or higher-status partners, while men prefer equal or lower-status partners  

## Page 361–380

- Humans focus more on negatives than positives  
- Resentment stems from exploitation or avoidance of responsibility  

## Page 381–400

- Overprotection harms development  
- Dependency undermines partnership  
- Fear of failure contributes to a lack of capable men  

## Page 401–420

- Nihilism emerges in the face of death  
- Limitation defines Being  
- Self-destruction worsens suffering  
- Noticing is more effective than overthinking  
- Perseverance is necessary through pain and loss
