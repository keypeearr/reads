# Life Mode On by Dr Joanne Orlando

## 10/02/2025
**Pages 13-25**

## 10/03/2025
**Pages 26-44**

- We hyperfocus technology itself, instead of our usage with it
- Three keys: it should serve us, the fulfillment it brings is different for everyone, and replacing an app or device won't change a thing
- Various conditions include: binge watching addiction, cyberchondria, cyberloafing, digital detox, and digital hypochondria
- Many conditions have been researched but they do not show causation, and they are likely late due to the fast advancements in technology
- Look for underlying causes for a specific behavior, many coin terminologies for disorders yet they have not looked at the underlying cause of it

## 10/06/2025
**Pages 45-71**

- Digital wellness is creating balance
- It's easy to get lost with the amount of misinformation and pseudoscience with regards to technology
- Be cautious when incorporating habits in your lifestyle that are blatant scams or fads
- Focus on creativity and empowerment instead of fear of usage
- Happiness is derived from the choices we make
- Good technology usage means building a relationship with technology that promotes belongingness, independence, productivity, and anything that makes a person happy
- Digital restriction is not liberation, stop avoiding the problem, instead look for ways to solve that problem
- Five actions for digital fulfillment: understand that technology can be a tool to enhance life, know your habits, know your context, recognize opportunities, and set goals
- Technology provides many assistance that it is impossible for a person to live without it
- Technology has become an extended mind for us
- Technology offers additional information and storage capacity
- Though it has its benefits, they also come with some disadvantages, such as weakening of brain function
- People often influenced by the behavior of experts than the content itself
- We live in an era that relies on knowledge, and when used well, technologies relieves the pressure to know them all

## 10/07/2025
**Pages 72-96**

- Technology often mislead people to more serious health issues and we humans tend to overstate the issue
- Intellectual arrogance comes from people not wanting to learn new things
- Children gain emotional security by using technology because it feels like an adult is nearby
- Our phone provides a sense of comfort
- Both successful and unsuccessful people use phones. The difference is in their habits in using their technology
- We use technology as an escape, it is junk food
- Relying on phones to escape our problems cause low level addiction and weakness in problem solving the daily problems in our lives
- Not everything that makes us happy makes us fulfilled
- Be intentional with your device setup
- Be intentional with your device floor plan

## 10/08/2025
**Pages 97-117**

- We are creatures of habit, there are no good and bad habits, only those that help you reach your goals or not
- Develop habits of creation through the use of technology instead of mindlessly scrolling through social media
- Use technology to successfully buffer negative emotions
- Technology causes low level stressors that eat us up
- Technology and the online world has become a crowded digital marketplace that causes disruption in our day to day lives
- Digital distractions take time away from quality decisions and effective communication
- All these notifications can make us feel like we have lost control, and without control we are powerless to pursue what we value

## 10/09/2025
**Pages 118-138**

- Digital stress is not about numbers it's about why we do what we do with technology
- We are having difficulty catching up due to information overload. This leads to us being more cynical than critical
- We are losing control over technology in terms of our usage and what we do with it
- Set boundaries
- Being conscious of others' usage of technology lessens our stress in toxic environments
- Social media taps into human connection, amplifying human condition
